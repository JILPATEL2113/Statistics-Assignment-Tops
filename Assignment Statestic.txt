MODULE 4: ESSENTIAL STATISTICS & MATHEMATICS FOR DATA SCIENCE

1.Explain Key Statistical Concepts:

Mean: Sum of all values divided by total number of values.
Median: Middle value after arranging data in ascending order.
Mode: Most frequently occurring value.
Variance: Average of squared differences from the mean.
Standard Deviation: Square root of variance.
Range: Difference between maximum and minimum values.
Skewness: Measure of asymmetry in data distribution.
Kurtosis: Measure of peaked Ness of distribution.
Correlation: Relationship between two variables.
Probability: Likelihood of an event happening.
Percentile: Value below which a certain percentage of data falls.
Quartile: Divides data into four equal parts (Q1, Q2, Q3).
Outlier: Value much smaller or larger than most of the data.
Hypothesis: Statement we test about the population.
Confidence Interval: Range likely to contain the true population value.

2.Importance of Probability in Machine Learning:

Helps models make decisions when data is uncertain.
Used in algorithms like Naive Bayes for predictions.
Shows how confident a model is in its prediction.
Helps choose the best action and estimate risks.

QUESTION 12: . A manufacturing company claims that the average weight of its product is 500grams. To test this claim, a random sample of 25 products is selected, and their
weights are measured. The sample mean weight is found to be 510 grams withasample standard deviation of 20 grams. Perform a hypothesis test to determineif
there is evidence to support the company's claim. Data: Sample size (n) =25, Sample mean (x̄) = 510 grams, Sample standard deviation (s) = 20 grams, Population mean (μ) = 500 grams

STEP 1: hypothesis

H₀: μ = 500
H₁: μ ≠ 500

STEP 2: formula

Since the sample size is small (n < 30) and population standard deviation is unknown, we use t-test:

t = (x̄ − μ) / (s / √n)

STEP 3: calculation

t = (510 − 500) / (20 / √25)
= 10 / (20 / 5)
= 10 / 4
= 2.5

STEP 4: DECISION
Degrees of freedom = n − 1 = 25 − 1 = 24
t critical (two-tailed, 95% confidence) ≈ 2.064

So ,2.5 > 2.064 → Reject H₀
CONCLUSION
There is enough evidence to say that the mean weight is not 500 grams. The company’s claim is not fully supported.

QUESTION 14:A survey is conducted to estimate the proportion of people in a city who support aparticular policy. A random sample of 500 individuals is surveyed, and 320of themexpress support for the policy. Calculate a 90% confidence interval for the
population proportion, given the sample proportion. Data: Sample size (n) =500, Number of successes (x) = 320, Confidence level = 90%

Confidence Interval for Population Proportion

Answer:

Given: n = 500, x = 320, p̂ = 0.64, Z = 1.645 (90% confidence)
Sample proportion, p̂ = x ÷ n = 320 ÷ 500 = 0.64
Confidence level = 90% → Z critical ≈ 1.645

Formula: CI = p̂ ± Z × √[p̂(1 − p̂)/n]
Standard error (SE) = √[p̂ × (1 − p̂) ÷ n]
Margin of error (ME) = Z × SE

Calculation:
SE = √[0.64 × 0.36 ÷ 500] ≈ 0.0215
ME = 1.645 × 0.0215 ≈ 0.0354
CI = 0.64 ± 0.0354 → 0.605 to 0.675

Answer: 90% CI = 0.605 to 0.675
we are 90% sure that about 61% to 68% of people in the city support the policy.
